Having a well-funded archive and team of researchers helps to make all needed information artifacts accessible and usable. Many missions though do not have these resources, and many have even more data. Who takes on the responsibilities of managing data long term?

"Best Practices" and standards differ between domains and missions

Always Consider:

Culture of practice

Institutional guidelines

Funder policies

There are no absolute solutions.
We are still defining the issues!
 

There are though some tools that can help and concepts to be well-aware of.

  • Create a Data Management Plan - think through the whole process
    • this should include realistic plans for post-mission data curation
  • Systematic version control is essential
  • File formats are relatively agreed upon (e.g. FITS) but components of those files' metadata change
    • Virtual Observatory standards and others exist
    • ​Understand that different types of instruments have very different standards though - spectrograph vs. an imager
  • ​Software is part of "the data" - ​Document full pipelines
  • Workflow support tools should be useful for as many parts of research process as possible
  • Make the work citable and archive it
    • Getting credit for this work will support sustainable funding models
    • "Software papers" are of emerging importance
    • Data hosting / warehousing cost models should be considered at the start
  • Remember Licenses! - http://choosealicense.com/
